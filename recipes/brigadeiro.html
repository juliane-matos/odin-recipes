Brigadeiro

Image by Larissa Kozemekin from Pixabay

A chocolate based sweet

This recipe is a tradition in brazilian birthdays party's, but also can be found in bakeries, coffee shops and restaurants. And it's so simple to prepare that it has become a comfort food all over the country, in a simplier version to be eaten with a spoon.

Ingredients

  • 1 can of condensed milk
  • 2 tablespoons of powder cocoa (minimum 50% cocoa, no sugar added)
  • 1 tablespoon of butter
  • Chocolate sprinkles (optional)

    • Directions

    1. Put all the ingredients in a saucepan.
    2. Cook and stir the mixture in medium heat until thickened.
    3. Transfer mixture to a bowl or plate and let it cool down.

    Brigadeiro is ready to eat with a spoon. But if you want the traditional rolled balls just follow the steps bellow:

    1. Pinch off some of the dough and roll into a small ball (if the dough is too sticky put some butter on hands before rolling the brigadeiros).
    2. Put the chocolate sprinkles in a bowl or plate and roll each brigadeiro in it.

    Enjoy it!

    Note: Brigadeiro pairs well with beijinho, another birthday parties clasic. It also can be used as a frosting for cakes, like the carrot cake. In this case just mix a can of creamy milk after the brigadeiro is cooked for a a perfect frosting texture.
